    • 1. Establishment of Efficient Technology for Combinatorial Biosynthesis

    Many biosynthetic genes in plants do not form gene cluster. Therefore, the localization of enzymes and protein-protein interactions are important for the efficient synthesis of plant natural products. Metabolon is one of the mechanisms in the plant to increase the efficiency of biosynthesis. When we perform the combinatorial biosynthesis, the enzymes from different sources are used to produce target products. Because it is difficult to make interaction between the enzymes from different organisms, other methods to improve the efficiency of metabolic pathway is required. To establish the efficient technology for combinatorial biosynthesis, we will focus on the Bacterial Micro-Compartment (BMC) to localize the enzymes. Bacterial microcompartments (BMCs) are self-assembling organelles that consist of an enzymatic core that is encapsulated by a selectively permeable protein shell. BMCs contain enzymes that catalyze sequential reactions and a private pool of cofactors within a protein shell. Because they self-assemble entirely from proteins, BMCs are becoming a viable platform for engineering novel nanoreactors.

    The recognition peptides to encapsulate have been identified. Therefore, the first, the recognition peptides will be engineered to improve the encapsulation. Then, the target enzymes will be fused with engineered leader peptides to localize the enzymes. The BMC of Salmonella enterica is related to ethanolamine utilization (Eut) and Propanediol utilization (Pdu).

    • 2. Target Isolation of Pyridinedione Alkaloids from Strain Phaeobotryon Cupressi 12020 Based on Genomic Information and LC-MS

    Fungi have high biodiversity and are an important source of natural products. In recent years, more than 50% of natural products with new structures and new activities come from fungi. Therefore, mining novel active compounds from fungi has been a hot topic in the field of natural product research. In the post-genomic era, genome mining strategies have revealed a large number of cryptic biosynthetic gene clusters and genomic mining has become increasingly important in the process of mining new natural products with the rapid development of high-throughput technologies. So how to achieve the efficient identification of novel compound structures from the prediction of unique biosynthetic gene clusters of natural products? It is of great importance to further establish two-dimensional NMR detection technology to directly identify nuclear magnetic signals related to the structure of new compounds from crude extracts. Based on this, we proposed a strategy that combines two-dimensional NMR and genomic information mining to study novel biosynthetic gene clusters and their corresponding products in fungi, in order to initially establish an efficient and rapid platform for identifying novel fungal compounds.
